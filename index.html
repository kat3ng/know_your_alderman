What is an Alderman?

Traditionally, "elder men" were high ranking officials in medievel England.

Today, Aldermen represent the interests of their ward residents and local communities.

In Chicago, there are 50 aldermen representing each of the 50 wards.

Alderman Responsibilites?

Aldermen make up the City Council, which serves as the local legislative branch.

City Council meets during monthly or weekly meetings to work on city policy which can include regulating: safety, public health, welfare, etc.

Aldermen also serve on committees for larger projects, such as building new schools or community centers.

Who can run for Alderman?

In the US, just about anyone can run for alderman.

Aldermen serve a term of 4 years.

The position can be either a full time job or a part time job and salary ranges between $10,000 and $100,000 in Chicago.

How can I vote for my Alderman?

In Chicago, the general election for mayor, city clerk, city treasurer, and all 50 aldermen will be February 26, 2019.

The deadline for candidates to file to run in this election is November 26, 2018.
